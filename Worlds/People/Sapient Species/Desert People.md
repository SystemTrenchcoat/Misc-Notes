## [[Gnolls]]

Practically hunger demons that only seek more food and land. They only care about taking more until there is nothing left, but they have (limited) thought, often only following their hunger


## [[Mummies]]

Often arising from fallen societies and tend to find kinship with each other, though some take enemies beyond the grave. The collective calls themselves "[[The Lost Kingdoms]]"


## [[Lizardfolk]]

They care for their craft of weapon and war. They use the entire body from the meat to the bones and view death as a noble end, for the body gets to be used for war. They live for war and conquest and view the world as strong and weak, predators and prey. They do have shamans to tap into the powers of the world, but they usually utilize them to wield the spirits as weapons in their wars.


## [[Yuan-Ti]]

Born of snake's cunning and beast power-lust, these serpentine creatures hunger for power endlessly. Formed from snakes in the beginning of the [[Age of Mortals]]. They understand that the gods, but don't really look to them for power, often choosing to look to their strongest as gods. The more blood they spill and swallow, the stronger they get. The stronger they get, the more they are able to alter themselves. Thus is the fuel behind their endless battles, each victory augmenting their strength, each failure bringing deeper knowledge.


## Sand [[Dwarves]]

Some of the more peaceful folk in the desert made up of primarily foragers and artisans. They protect themselves as much as they must, but they are primarily merchants and traders, earning their protection through their goods, but fighting when necessary.


## Humans

Human kingdoms in the desert are often protected by a [[Sphinx]], allowing them to study the desert to their hearts' content. The humans in these towns see knowledge as power, and are widely philosophers, artificers, and mages.


## [[Dragonkin]]

Often a part of either a fire or spirit dragon's horde. Regardless, they fight to grow their respective dragons' power, sometimes through combat, but often through sweet talk and deals, convincing people to join (like an MLM).


## [[Birdfolk]]

A group of them that followed [[Caliel]] to make their way in the wastes. Through lies and deceit, they got some land and power, but it costed them their flight and voice.


## [[Catfolk]]

[[Beastfolk]] with autistic levels of fascination on the most random of things. They are skilled hunters, ambush predator that use guerrilla tactics to defend themselves and their homes. Most are content with the land they have, but a few try to earn more, scoping out a new location before planning a stealthy attack.


## [[Scorpi]]

Solitary creatures who embody survivability. Modeled after scorpions, they seek to understand themselves and where they stand in the universe. The deeper their understanding, the more they're able to alter their being, usually growing more scorpion like, but occasionally something else. They are also biologically immortal, the only known mortals with this trait. They only fight when necessary, but are quite naturally adept at it.


## [[Kharn]]

Thought by some to be sent by Caparicus ([[Eido]] and [[Animus]]) to usher spirits across to the afterlife and thought by others to be sent by Kyrono ([[Sapienta]] and [[Pagamarous]]) to herald war. Regardless of the interpretation, it is certain that they are divinely sent. Coming in twins, they stay at and protect their holy lands, only leaving on divine quests.


## [[Irwi]]

[[Birdfolk]] that devoted themselves to the teachings of a deity, usually one of knowledge or war. Those devoted to knowledge tend to find a place to study and defend it with their lives. Those devoted to war in contrast conquest for more and more land

## [[Vyeir]]

Lizardfolk with a powerful [[Pyr|pyr]] and [[Ner|ner]] elements. While lizardfolk traditionally have no emotion to speak of, [[Vyeir]] have potent emotions that change on a dime. They tend to be conquerors, taking over more lands to chase their passions. They are a lot like demons, seeing only a need to satisfy their desire, led by passion and emotion


## [[Qua]]

Nomads that live in the canyons, not really seeking land ownership, but fighting for their right to walk their canyons. They are the most peaceful of all the desert people, openly avoiding conflicts when possible, though when it comes down to it, they are formidable fighters.
